# Policy: MRI Lumbar Spine — Prior Authorization Criteria
**Policy ID:** POL-MRI-LS-001  
**Version:** 2.1  
**Effective Date:** 2024-01-01  

## Overview
MRI of the lumbar spine requires prior authorization. 
Approval is granted when medical necessity criteria are met.

## Approval Criteria
ALL of the following must be documented:

1. **Duration of symptoms:** Low back pain persisting for 6 weeks or more 
   despite conservative treatment.
2. **Conservative therapy:** Patient has completed at least 6 weeks of 
   conservative therapy including at least TWO of the following:
   - Physical therapy (minimum 6 sessions)
   - NSAIDs or analgesic medications
   - Chiropractic care
   - Rest and activity modification
3. **Clinical indication:** At least ONE of the following must be present:
   - Radiculopathy with neurological deficits (weakness, numbness, reflex changes)
   - Suspicion of serious pathology (infection, malignancy, fracture)
   - Cauda equina syndrome symptoms
   - Failure to improve after completing conservative therapy

## Denial Criteria
Request will be DENIED if:
- Symptoms present for less than 6 weeks without red flag indicators
- No documentation of conservative therapy attempt
- MRI previously performed within 12 months with no new clinical findings

## Missing Information — Request More Info If:
- Duration of symptoms not documented
- Conservative therapy details are absent or incomplete
- No neurological exam findings documented

## Notes
Urgent/emergent requests (cauda equina, suspected malignancy, fracture) 
bypass standard criteria and are approved with clinical documentation.
